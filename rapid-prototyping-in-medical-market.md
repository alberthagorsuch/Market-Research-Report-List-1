<p><ul><li>Stereolithogrphy Apparatus (SLA)</li><li>Laminated Object Manufacturing (LOM)</li><li>Selective Laser Sintering (SLS)</li><li>Three Dimension Printing (3DP)</li><li>Fused Depostion Modeling (FDM)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Rapid prototyping in the medical market utilizes various technologies to create accurate, functional models for testing and development. Stereolithography (SLA) employs UV light to solidify liquid resin, producing high-resolution prototypes. Laminated Object Manufacturing (LOM) layers adhesive sheets to form 3D shapes, ideal for creating large models. Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) uses lasers to fuse powdered materials, enabling strong and complex structures. Three-Dimensional Printing (3DP) jets bonding agents onto powdered materials for quick assembly, while Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) extrudes thermoplastic filaments to build durable prototypes.</p></p>
