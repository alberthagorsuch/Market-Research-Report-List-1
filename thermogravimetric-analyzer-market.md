<p><p>The thermogravimetric analyzer (TGA) market is segmented into high-pressure and atmospheric TGAs. High-pressure TGAs operate under elevated pressures, enabling the analysis of materials in conditions similar to industrial processes, making them suitable for studying reactions in gases or liquids. Atmospheric TGAs, on the other hand, function at ambient pressure, focusing on temperature-based weight changes, ideal for conventional thermal stability studies. Each type serves distinct applications, catering to specific research requirements in materials science, pharmaceuticals, and chemical industries.</p></p>
