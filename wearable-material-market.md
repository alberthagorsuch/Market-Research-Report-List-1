<p><p>The wearable material market is primarily categorized into three types: silicones, polyurethanes, and fluoroelastomers. Silicones are valued for their flexibility, durability, and skin-friendliness, making them ideal for wearable devices and medical applications. Polyurethanes offer excellent elasticity and abrasion resistance, suitable for high-performance sportswear and wearable tech. Fluoroelastomers, known for their chemical resistance and thermal stability, are used in more specialized applications, including advanced sensors and medical devices, providing enhanced longevity and reliability in challenging environments.</p></p>
