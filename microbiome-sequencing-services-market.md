<p><p>Microbiome sequencing services encompass various technologies for analyzing microbial populations. Sequencing by synthesis is a widely used method that incorporates fluorescent labeled nucleotides to identify DNA sequences. Sequencing by ligation involves the use of specific oligonucleotides that bind to target sequences, allowing for precise readings of DNA fragments. Sanger sequencing, a traditional method, utilizes chain-terminating inhibitors to produce readable fragment lengths, offering high accuracy for small-scale sequencing projects. Together, these methods enhance our understanding of microbiomes in health and disease.</p></p>
