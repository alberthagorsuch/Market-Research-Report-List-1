<p><p>The electrolysis method for producing solid caustic soda primarily involves two market types: the Ion Exchange Membrane Method and the Diaphragm Electrolysis Method. The Ion Exchange Membrane Method utilizes a selective membrane to separate the anode and cathode compartments, ensuring high purity caustic soda with reduced energy consumption. In contrast, the Diaphragm Electrolysis Method employs a porous diaphragm to prevent the mixing of products, offering a simpler design but potentially lower purity and energy efficiency. Both methods are crucial for meeting industrial demand.</p></p>
